With the exception of Puerto Rico, there is a moderate positive correlation between population poverty percentage vs population percentage lacking healthcare.

There are some interesting findings:

  • Texas has highest percentage of people lacking healthcare, yet it does not have highest poverty ratio.
  • Massachusetts has lowest percentage of pople lacking healthcare and some other factor such as statewide mandartory insurance is playing here.
  • New Hampshire has lowest poverty ratio, but it stands middle in term of % lacking healthcare.
